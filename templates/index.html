Full Name

Enter your full name for the medical record.

Age

Your current age in years. Heart disease risk increases with age, especially after 45 for men and 55 for women.

Sex

Your biological sex. Males statistically have a higher risk of heart disease.

Chest Pain Type

The type of chest pain you experience during daily activities or exercise.

Resting Blood Pressure

Your blood pressure (mm Hg) when you're idle and at rest — for example, lying on your bed. Normal is around 120; above 140 is considered high.

Cholesterol

Your serum cholesterol level in mg/dl. This is the total cholesterol in your blood. Values above 200 mg/dl are considered borderline high.

Fasting Blood Sugar

Whether your fasting blood sugar is above 120 mg/dl. High blood sugar can damage blood vessels over time.

Resting ECG

Results of your resting electrocardiogram — measures your heart's electrical activity at rest.

Max Heart Rate

The maximum heart rate achieved during exercise (bpm). A lower max heart rate during exertion can indicate heart problems. Typical range: 60–202 bpm.

Exercise-Induced Angina

Whether physical exercise triggers chest pain. Exercise angina suggests reduced blood flow to the heart during activity.

Oldpeak (ST Depression)

ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest. This measures how much the heart's electrical signal drops during exercise. Higher values may indicate ischemia. Typical range: 0–6.2.

Slope

The slope of the peak exercise ST segment on your ECG reading.

CA (Major Vessels)

Number of major blood vessels colored by fluoroscopy. More colored vessels mean better blood flow.

Thalassemia

A blood disorder that affects hemoglobin and oxygen delivery to your heart.
