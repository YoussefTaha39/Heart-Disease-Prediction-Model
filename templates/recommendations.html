Moderate Concern, {{ name }} — Take Action Now

Your results indicate a moderate risk. With some lifestyle changes, you can significantly lower your chances:

🩺

See Your Doctor

Schedule an appointment for a full cardiac check-up. Discuss your results and get personalized medical advice.

🚭

Quit Smoking

If you smoke, quitting is the single most impactful thing you can do. Smoking doubles the risk of heart disease.

🧂

Reduce Sodium

Limit salt intake to under 2,300 mg/day. High sodium raises blood pressure, a major heart disease risk factor.

🏋️

Increase Activity

Aim for 30 minutes of moderate exercise daily. Even walking after meals can improve blood sugar and blood pressure.

⚖️

Manage Weight

If overweight, losing even 5–10% of body weight can reduce cholesterol, blood pressure, and blood sugar levels.

🧘

Stress Management

Chronic stress contributes to heart disease. Practice meditation, deep breathing, or hobbies to manage stress levels.

{% else %}

Urgent, {{ name }} — Consult a Doctor Immediately

Your results suggest a high probability of heart disease. Please take the following steps seriously:

🚨

Immediate Medical Attention

Contact your doctor or visit a cardiologist as soon as possible. Do not ignore chest pain, shortness of breath, or fatigue.

💊

Medication Review

Your doctor may prescribe statins for cholesterol, ACE inhibitors for blood pressure, or aspirin therapy. Follow their guidance strictly.

🫀

Cardiac Testing

Request an ECG, echocardiogram, or stress test. These can reveal blockages, valve issues, or abnormal heart rhythms.

🚭

Quit Smoking & Alcohol

Both smoking and excessive alcohol significantly increase heart disease risk. Seek help programs if needed.

🥦

Heart-Healthy Diet

Follow the DASH or Mediterranean diet. Focus on omega-3 fatty acids (fish, nuts), fiber, and potassium-rich foods.

📋

Monitor Daily

Track your blood pressure, heart rate, and symptoms daily. Keep a log to share with your healthcare provider.
